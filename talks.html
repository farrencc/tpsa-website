    Talks

    Throughout the academic year, the TPSA hosts talks by experts in their respective fields, providing our members with insights into cutting-edge research and the broader applications of theoretical physics. These presentations offer students the opportunity to engage with leading researchers and explore diverse areas of physics beyond the standard curriculum.

    Measuring the Strength of the Strong Nuclear Force

    Professor Stefan Sint - DIAS

    In this video, Professor Stefan Sint provides an in-depth overview of key topics related to measuring the strong nuclear force. This talk unpacks the theory, tools, and ongoing questions physicists face in decoding the strong interaction.

    What Quantum Theories Teach us About Gravity

    Professor Manuela Kulaxizi - Trinity College Dublin

    In this video, Professor Manuela Kulaxizi tells us what we can learn about gravity using quantum theories. Enjoy the exploration from the smallest parts of the universe to the largest!

    Simulating Stochastic Processes with Quantum Physics

    Professor Felix Binder - Trinity College Dublin

    In this TPSA talk, Professor Felix Binder explains how to simulate stochastic processes with quantum physics.

    A Theory of Everything Integrable: Self-Dual Yang-Mills

    Professor Tristan McLoughlin - Trinity College Dublin

    This collaboration between the TPSA and DU Mathsoc is a talk by Professor Tristan McLoughlin on self-dual Yang-Mills theory, and how it is a potential theory of everything (integrable).

    Extreme Explosions and Catastrophic Collisions

    Professor Kate Maguire - Trinity College Dublin

    This TPSA talk by Professor Kate Maguire is about extreme explosions and catastrophic collisions in the universe. Topics discussed include different types of supernovae, as well as gravitational waves and methods astrophysicists use to detect them.

    Investigating Quark Confinement by QCD on the Computer

    Professor Mike Peardon - Trinity College Dublin

    This TPSA talk by Professor Mike Peardon is about using high performance computing to investigate lattice QCD problems.

    From Birr to Boolardy: The Universe at 1000 FPS

    Professor Evan Keane - Trinity College Dublin

    This TPSA talk by Professor Evan Keane is about the universe at 1000 frames per second, and how this can be used to study astrophysical phenomena.

    Black Hole Entropy in Supergravity

    Professor Manya Sahni - Trinity College Dublin

    The TPSA talk by Prof. Manya Sahni is about black hole entropy in supergravity.
